Our story

From the comal of Mexico City to Flagey

Comal was born when Diego and Margaux came back to Brussels — two partners who first met in a neighbourhood diner in Mexico City. He had grown up there, between his grandmother's comal (the cast-iron griddle where tortillas are cooked) and the markets of Roma; she had gone to learn how to cook and never quite came back the same. From those shared years they kept a single obsession: making, in Brussels, the tacos you eat standing up, at night, in the streets of Mexico City.

At Comal, everything starts with the masa. The corn is nixtamalised then pressed fresh on site every morning: no industrial tortilla, no shortcut. The rest follows the same logic — cochinita pibil marinated for twelve hours, al pastor carved off the spit, salsas made to order. The produce is as Belgian as it can be: market gardeners from Brabant, free-range pork, herbs from a small grower in Jette.

The dining room looks the way we like it: raw concrete, warm neon, tables elbow to elbow, and a mezcalería where you take your time. You come for the tacos, you stay for the mezcal — that's pretty much the whole plan.
